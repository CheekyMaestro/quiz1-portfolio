About My Hometown

Surabaya, known as the "City of Heroes" (Kota Pahlawan), is the capital of East Java and Indonesia's second-largest city. The city earned its heroic title from the pivotal role it played in Indonesia's independence struggle, particularly during the Battle of Surabaya on November 10, 1945.

As a major industrial and business center, Surabaya is home to numerous universities, including my alma mater Institut Teknologi Sepuluh Nopember (ITS). The city perfectly blends modern development with rich cultural heritage, offering everything from historic sites to contemporary shopping centers.

What I love most about Surabaya is its vibrant street food culture, friendly people, and the perfect balance between urban convenience and traditional charm. It's a city that never sleeps, always bustling with activity and opportunity.

Culture & Lifestyle

üé≠ Cultural Heritage

Surabaya is a melting pot of Javanese, Madurese, Chinese, and Arab cultures. This diversity is reflected in the city's architecture, traditions, and especially its culinary scene.

  • Ludruk - Traditional Javanese comedy performance
  • Reog - Traditional dance from East Java
  • Wayang - Shadow puppet performances
